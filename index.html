What about me?

I'm a French expat/immigrant who's decided at 30 that he wants to be a developer. Before, I've worked all sorts of jobs, from manual labor to business development to sales, retail, and customer service management. I have little experience coding but a lot of soft skills picked up along my career. And I'll put in the work to make that reconversion successful.

So why the career change?

I worked in startups before, not as, but with developers and that got me interested in their role within a company. My previous missions were operational, sometimes strategic, and for the most part, interesting. But I'm interested in working on the product. Actually creating the product, getting your hands dirty. And the process of it, the problem solving, the continuous learning, are icing on the cake. That's what developers do. That's what I want to do.
