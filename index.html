So why the career change?

My underwhelming German ( but I'm working on it) and my personal interests me naturally steered me towards working for startups. There, I got to work with teams of developers ( usually as the guy bringing up customer complaints ) and figuratively look over the shoulder of my colleagues. And I wanted in. I really liked my previous jobs, and most of the people I worked with, but managing a team of customer service agents is very day to day, operational stuff. Bringing in issues, and talking about them on a theoretical level is just not enough. I like getting my hands dirty, being part of the creation process and in startups, that means being a developer. That's what got me interested. Then, the problem solving, the continuous learning, the intellectual stimulation tipped me over. When my company went out of business last summer ( though not because of corona), I decided to give it a go.

What do I bring to the table?

I want to make a career change, and I'm not getting any younger. I do not have the best developing skills in town, but I have some coding skills, and a strong desire to make that career change a successful one. I will work hard, receive feedback eagerly and apply any advice I will receive. And I like to think I did pick up some soft skills during the 13 years I've been working ( I did not least every single summer job on my resume as it would be quite a tedious read), soft skills that will make me a pleasure to work with, I swear. I followed Le Wagon, a full stack development bootcamp focused on Ruby on Rails, with a zest of Javascript. It was quite intense. I got to learn HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, hosting, Scraping, working as a team on Git, building relational databases with SQL. I also got to code and build a few apps, solo and in teams, that you'll be able to consult on my portfolio page
