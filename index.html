Hi! I'm Louis.

This website is still under construction. Watch your head for falling mortar and don't mind the occasional typos.
I started a career change a few months ago and decided to learn coding. I am now eager to get some real experience and I'm looking for an internship, or a junior position as a web developer. I am based in Berlin, but open to working remotely.

What about me?

I'm a French expat/immigrant who's decided at 30 that he wants to be a developer. Before, I've worked all sorts of jobs, from manual labor to business development to sales, retail and customer service management. I have little experience coding but a lot of soft skills picked up along my career. And I'll put in the work to make that reconversion successful.

So why the career change?

I worked in startups before, not as, but with developers and that got me interested in their role within a company. My previous missions were operational, sometimes strategic, and for the most part, interesting. But I'm interested in working on the product. Actually creating the product, getting your hands dirty. And the process of it, the problem solving, the continuous learning, are icing on the cake. That's what developers do. That's what I want to do.

I went through a bootcamp, and did some self learning.

Le Wagon bootcamp

During this 9 week bootcamp, I was taught the core concepts of coding, before having to put them in practice to solve coding challenges at first, and to then to code Ruby on Rails apps.

This bootcamp focuses on Ruby on Rails, with a zest of Javascript. It was quite intense. I got to learn HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, hosting, Scraping, working as a team on Git, building relational databases with SQL. I also got to code and build a few apps, solo and in teams, that you'll be able to consult on my portfolio page.

Javascript30

I am currently going through Wes Bos's Javascript 30 days course. To put it in his words, this course is about getting comfortable with Vanilla JS fundamentals, by building things, and through repetition.
